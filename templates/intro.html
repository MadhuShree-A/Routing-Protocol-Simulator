What is Routing?

Routing is the process of selecting the best path for data to travel from a source to a destination across a network.

When you send an email, load a webpage, or stream a video, your data is broken into packets. These packets must travel through multiple routers and networks to reach the destination.

Types of Routing Algorithms

Link-State Routing

Each router has a complete view of the network and computes the shortest path.

OSPF

Distance-Vector Routing

Routers share information only with neighbors and update routing tables iteratively.

RIP
